I'm Jake. I build backend and distributed systems for a living. Larkin Labs is a blog where I occasionally write about software, architecture, AI, side projects, home improvement work, things I'm picking up about adjacent disciplines, and whatever else seems worth keeping a record of.

I grew up in New York City, spent my early adulthood in the San Francisco Bay Area and now reside in the Eastside suburbs of Seattle. I studied 'Mathematical and Computational Science' at Stanford - a fun catchall of Applied Math, Statistics and Computer Science. After that I briefly worked as a financial data analyst, before getting a Master's in Computer Engineering. I started my Software Engineering career as a backend developer at Strava back when GPS-based fitness apps were figuring out how to scale. At Uber, I worked on the ML platform, adding features that let teams train and deploy state-of-the-art neural network models, and was the lead developer on the real-time streaming API serving the mobile app. Finally at AWS I worked on a team managing a large database platform (Keyspaces) and tech-leading a new initiative for data migration.
